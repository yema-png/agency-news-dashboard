About & Roadmap

01

What This Is

This is AndIron Group's internal weekly news intelligence dashboard. It automatically monitors the news landscape for each client and surfaces the most relevant stories, trends, and talking points ahead of WIP meetings — saving the team hours of manual monitoring every week.

Rather than searching Google News manually or relying on ad-hoc media alerts, this dashboard pulls from over 150,000 publications, scores every article for relevance using Claude AI, and presents the results in a format ready to use in client conversations.

02

How It Works Right Now

News Source
NewsAPI — 150,000+ global and Australian publications indexed in real time
Refresh Cadence
Manual — click Refresh All in the sidebar or the per-client refresh button on each client page
AI Processing
Claude AI reads each article, scores it for relevance (0–10), writes a one-line summary, and generates a meeting angle — a ready-to-use talking point for client WIPs
Caching
Results are cached locally so the dashboard loads instantly. Only a manual refresh hits the APIs — no unnecessary spend between meetings
Client Config
Each client has a profile in config.json with keywords, topics, scope (AU or global), and plain-English context about their business — this is what tells Claude what's relevant

03

How Relevance Scoring Works

Every article is scored by Claude on a scale of 0–10 before it appears in the dashboard. Articles below 5 are filtered out entirely and never shown.

8 – 10
High relevance. Directly mentions the client, their product, their named technology, or a market event that immediately affects them. Prime candidate for WIP discussion or proactive media pitch.
5 – 7
Industry relevant. Relevant to the client's sector, a topic they track, or a trend they have a stake in — even if the client isn't named. Good for background briefing and messaging context.
0 – 4
Filtered out. Not relevant enough to surface. Claude drops these before they reach the dashboard.

Each client also has a relevance context field in their config — plain-English guidance that tells Claude how to calibrate scoring for that specific client. For example, Sunshine Hydro's context instructs Claude to score any Queensland energy transition story highly, even if Sunshine Hydro isn't named directly.

How to Update Client Config

Everything about how a client is tracked — their keywords, topics, scope, and context — lives in a single file: config.json. You never need to touch any code directly.

1
Open Claude Code in the terminal from the project folder (agency-news-dashboard).
2
Describe the change in plain English. For example: "Add 'carbon credits' and 'emissions trading' to Sunshine Hydro's keywords" or "Update Discovery Consulting's description — they now also serve healthcare clients."
3
Claude Code updates config.json directly. No restart needed — the new config takes effect on the next refresh.
4
Click Refresh for that client (or Refresh All) to pull news using the updated profile.
To add a completely new client, just tell Claude Code the client's name, what they do, their industry, and any key topics to track. It will create the full config entry including keywords, topics, scope, context, and relevance guidance.
